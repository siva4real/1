Code Sample: Revenue Projection


def calculate_projection(current_revenue, growth_rate):
  """Calculates next quarter's revenue projection."""
  return current_revenue * (1 + growth_rate)

projected_revenue = calculate_projection(150, 0.05)
print(f"Projected Revenue: ${projected_revenue}M")
Here's a small Python snippet demonstrating how we calculate our revenue projections. We are expecting a 5% growth in the next quarter.

Financial Formula

We use the following formula to calculate Compound Annual Growth Rate (CAGR):

\[ \text{CAGR} = \left( \frac{\text{Ending Value}}{\text{Beginning Value}} \right)^{\frac{1}{\text{Number of Years}}} - 1 \]

This is the standard formula we use for calculating CAGR, which gives us a clear picture of our long-term growth.
